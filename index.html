Lead With A Story

A Guide to Crafting Business Narratives Taht Captivate, Convince and Inspire
by Paul Smith

Summary

Lead with a Story is a highly engaging book proves that stories sell. In it Paul Smith shares that stories actively involve people in the communication process, which results in the desired outcome of the speaker.

Apart from explaining how to use a story, Smith also explains how to create or borrow one. Each point is appropriately made with a story, along with alot of facts and figures spread throughout the book. Stories help the listener to experience and learn from the teller's challenge and/or solution.

Structure of A Story

If you ask a child, What's the order of a good story? they'll respond with Oh, that's easy, there's a beginning, a middle and an end. If you ask a script writer the same question they might respond and say there are six parts:

the set up, catalyst, first turning point, climax, final confrontation and resolution.

Every answer will differ from person to person, therefore all a leader needs is a simple structure that works. Everyone is a natural-born storyteller, since we grew up listening to bedtime stories, which helped us to know what's a good structure for a story.

A useful tip to for remembering the basic struture of a story is to use CAR

Context- context provides all the necessary background for he story to make sense. If done right, it generates interest and excitement in the audience making want to hear the rest of the story.
Action- this is where you tell the ups and downs that happen to the main character. These help build excitement in the story.
Result- this is the final stage where you not only explain the right lesson the audience should've learned, but also the reason why you told the story in the first place.
